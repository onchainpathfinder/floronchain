# FLOR Onchain — Brand Context Reference

## Brand Identity
- **Site:** floronchain.com (also lives as the ONCHAIN pillar on followtheflor.com)
- **Author:** Flor (Floriana, based in Malta)
- **Mission:** Make crypto compliance, iGaming regulation, and AI/automation in regulated industries accessible to everyone — from compliance officers to crypto founders to degens who just want to understand the rules
- **Positioning:** A compliance expert who actually works in this space, explaining regulation in plain English without dumbing it down

## Audience Segments (by priority for monetisation)

### Tier 1: Compliance professionals
- MLROs, compliance analysts, risk managers in crypto/iGaming
- They want: practical guidance, regulatory updates, red flag libraries, templates they can adapt
- They'll pay for: deep guides, toolkits, courses, consulting

### Tier 2: Crypto founders & operators
- Building exchanges, DeFi protocols, payment platforms, NFT marketplaces
- They want: "what do I actually need to do to be compliant?" in plain language
- They'll pay for: compliance readiness guides, MiCA prep content, consultation

### Tier 3: Crypto-curious & degens
- People who use crypto but don't understand the regulatory landscape
- They want: "what does this regulation mean for ME?" explained without jargon
- They monetise through: volume, newsletter growth, affiliate potential, community building

## Topics Covered
- **Crypto AML/CFT** — red flags, typologies, transaction monitoring, suspicious activity
- **MiCA** — EU Markets in Crypto-Assets regulation, implementation timelines, requirements by entity type
- **iGaming regulation** — gambling compliance, licensing, cross-border issues
- **Web3 compliance** — DeFi, DAOs, NFTs, stablecoins from a regulatory lens
- **AI & automation in compliance** — tools, workflows, how AI changes the compliance landscape
- **Regulation explainers** — new laws, consultations, enforcement actions, what they mean in practice

## Voice Rules
- **Tone:** Professional and authoritative, but human. Not academic, not corporate.
- **Style:** Clear, direct. Explain complexity without oversimplifying. Respect the reader's intelligence.
- **The difference from followtheflor:** Less personal storytelling, more expert analysis. Still Flor's voice — still direct, still occasionally irreverent — but the expertise leads, not the lifestyle.
- **Italian/Maltese language:** Use sparingly here. An occasional "allura" is fine; this brand leans more international.

### NEVER write:
- Dense legal text with no explanation
- "It depends" without then explaining what it depends on
- Clickbait regulatory fear-mongering ("CRYPTO IS ABOUT TO BE BANNED")
- Content that could be mistaken for legal advice (always disclaim)
- Generic summaries that add nothing beyond what the regulation text says

### ALWAYS write:
- What it actually means in practice — not just what the law says
- Who it affects and what they need to do
- Specific examples, scenarios, red flags
- Clear structure: the reader should be able to scan H2s and get the gist
- A disclaimer that this is educational, not legal advice

## Content Formats

### Long-form articles (800-1500 words)
Main content type. Deep enough to be useful, short enough to finish.
Structure: Hook → Context → The actual substance → What to do about it → CTA

### Quick explainers (400-600 words)
"X explained in 5 minutes" format. Single concept, no tangents.
Structure: What is it → Why it matters → What happens next

### Red flag guides
Specific, categorised red flag indicators for a particular typology or sector.
Structure: Overview → Red flags by category (tables) → Real-world example (fictitious data) → What to do if you spot it

### Regulation breakdowns
When new regulation drops or a consultation closes.
Structure: What changed → Who's affected → Timeline → What to do now → Flor's take

### AI & automation pieces
How AI/automation intersects with compliance, crypto, iGaming.
Structure: The problem → How AI/automation addresses it → Limitations/risks → Practical implementation

## SEO Rules (same fundamentals as followtheflor)
- Keyword plan BEFORE writing (primary + 2-5 secondary)
- Primary keyword in: title, H1, first 100 words, one H2, meta description
- Title tag: 50-60 characters
- Meta description: 140-160 characters with CTA
- Every image has descriptive alt text
- Internal links to related content (both floronchain and followtheflor where relevant)

## Distribution Channels (priority order)

### 1. X/Twitter (PRIMARY)
This is where the crypto compliance conversation happens. Format:
- Thread (5-8 tweets): Hook → key points → link to full article
- Single tweet: bold take or stat + link
- Quote-tweet regulatory news with Flor's analysis

### 2. Newsletter (Beehiiv)
Educational series. Links back to full articles. Builds owned audience.
- Weekly or biweekly cadence
- 2-3 paragraphs with one key insight + link to full piece

### 3. YouTube (when ready)
Explainer videos. Same structure as articles but adapted for screen.
- 5-10 minute format
- Script follows: hook (10 sec) → context (60 sec) → substance (bulk) → CTA (10 sec)

### 4. LinkedIn (OPTIONAL — use cautiously)
Only for genuinely educational content that reads as professional development, not personal branding. Keep it neutral enough that a boss would approve.
- Max 150 words
- No "I'm building my brand" energy — pure value
- 3-5 short paragraphs, end with a question

## Legal Disclaimer
Every article must end with a variation of:
> *This content is for educational and informational purposes only. It does not constitute legal, financial, or compliance advice. Always consult a qualified professional for advice specific to your situation.*

Keep the wording natural — not identical every time. But it must always be there.
