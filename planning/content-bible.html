LAUNCH TARGET

YOUR BRAND VOICE — THE RULES

THE TONE

Bloomberg meets crypto Twitter. Smart, direct, slightly sharp. You're not performing — you're telling. Like a brilliant friend who happens to be a compliance expert.

"I file STRs for a living. Here's what criminal crypto actually looks like."

HOOK FORMULA

Every video opens with ONE of: a shocking number, a bold claim, or a question that makes people feel slightly anxious. NEVER "In today's video we're going to talk about..."

"$75 billion. That's how much was laundered through crypto last year. Let me show you how."

FIRST PERSON ALWAYS

You're not a narrator. You're a practitioner sharing what you know. Use "In my experience", "What I actually see", "I've filed reports on exactly this."

"I've reviewed hundreds of suspicious transactions. This pattern shows up every time."

THE COMPLIANCE ANGLE

It's your unfair advantage. Every topic gets the compliance layer that no one else gives. Not heavy-handed — just: "and here's what this means if you're a compliance professional."

"For everyday users this is great. For compliance teams — here's your new headache."

MAKE IT VISCERAL

Use real numbers. Real cases. Real names (when documented). Make abstract concepts feel like something that happened to a real person.

"A hospital in Germany. $1.3 million in Bitcoin. Paid to criminals. In 24 hours."

END WITH YOUR TAKE

Every video ends with 30–45 seconds of YOUR opinion. Not a summary — your honest view. This is what makes people subscribe: they want to know what Flor actually thinks.

"Honestly? I think this regulation is 3 years too late. But here's why it might still work."

SENTENCE LENGTH

Short. Punchy. Pause after a big statement. Let it land. Then continue. Your delivery should feel like each sentence was chosen deliberately.

"It's free. It takes 60 seconds. And it's caught more rug pulls than any paid tool I've used."

WHAT YOU NEVER SAY

"In today's rapidly evolving landscape..." / "It's important to note that..." / "As we can see..." / "To summarise..." — banned. Cut them every time.

Instead of "It's important to note the regulatory implications" → "Here's what this means for compliance."

What Is a Hardware Wallet? (And Why Leaving Crypto on an Exchange Is a Gamble)
8–10 min Beginners WhiteboardCrypto style
SEO
Primary keyword: what is a hardware wallet · Secondary: Ledger wallet explained, cold wallet vs hot wallet, how to store crypto safely, Ledger setup beginners · Intent: Informational (beginner)
FULL SCRIPT
HOOK — 0:00–0:08 · Direct to camera · Hold up your Ledger device

"This little thing. It costs €80. And it could save you from losing everything you own in crypto."

[2-second pause. Let it land.]

"It's called a hardware wallet. And if you have crypto sitting on an exchange right now — you need to understand what this is."

📱 TEXT: "Hardware wallet — the most important device in crypto"

CONTEXT — 0:08–1:00 · Cut to screen recording of FTX headline

"In November 2022, FTX collapsed. Overnight. 8 million customers lost access to their funds. Some of them lost everything they had."

"FTX was one of the largest exchanges in the world. Trusted by everyone. Gone in 72 hours."

"But here's the thing. The people who had their crypto in a hardware wallet? They lost nothing. Because their crypto was never on FTX to begin with."

📱 TEXT: "FTX: $8 billion lost. Hardware wallet users: $0 lost."

EXPLANATION 1 — 1:00–2:30 · Camera · Use hand gestures

"Okay, let me break this down simply. When you buy crypto on an exchange — Coinbase, Binance, Kraken — you don't technically own that crypto. You own an IOU."

[pause]

"Think of it like this. Imagine you give your cash to a friend to hold. You trust them. But if your friend gets robbed, or disappears, or decides they want to keep your money — that cash is gone. And you have no legal right to get it back."

"That's what it means to keep crypto on an exchange. Not your keys, not your coins. This phrase? It's the most important sentence in crypto."

📱 TEXT: "Not your keys, not your coins."

EXPLANATION 2 — 2:30–4:00 · Camera + graphic animation if possible

"A hardware wallet changes this completely. Instead of the exchange holding your crypto, YOU hold it. The device generates and stores something called a private key — a secret code that proves you own your assets. And it never leaves the device."

"Think of the hardware wallet like a safe. A physical, offline safe. Even if every exchange in the world collapsed tomorrow — your safe is still there. Your crypto is still there. Because it was never on their server."

📱 TEXT: "Private key = your ownership proof. Never leave the device."

HOW IT WORKS — 4:00–6:00 · Screen recording — hands on Ledger

[Hold up Ledger device to camera]

"This is a Ledger Nano X. Most popular hardware wallet in the world. Let me show you how it actually works."

"When you first set it up, it gives you a 24-word recovery phrase. This is the most important thing I will tell you today: write these 24 words down on paper. Store the paper somewhere safe. Never photograph it. Never type it anywhere. Never, ever enter it on any website."

"Those 24 words ARE your wallet. Anyone who has those words has your crypto. Anyone. Including people who work at Ledger. The device itself is just a tool — the 24 words are the actual key."

📱 TEXT: "24-word seed phrase = NEVER share this. With anyone. Ever."

[pause. Look directly at camera]

"I'll say that one more time. There is no customer support team that will ever ask for your seed phrase. If anyone asks for it — it's a scam. Full stop."

DEMO — 6:00–7:30 · Screen recording — Ledger Live app

"Day to day, you manage it through an app called Ledger Live. You can see your balances, send and receive crypto, and connect to DeFi apps. All of it is confirmed on the device with a physical button press — so even if your computer is hacked, the transaction can't go through without your confirmation."

"And here's what makes it so powerful from a security standpoint. The private key is stored in a secure chip inside the device. It never touches the internet. It never touches your computer. Hackers can't steal what they can never see."

📱 TEXT: "Private key = offline. Always. Hackers can't steal what isn't online."

FLOR'S TAKE — 7:30–8:30 · Camera · Honest, direct

"Now, I need to be honest with you about something. A hardware wallet is not for everyone — yet."

"If you're buying £50 of crypto to test it out, leaving it on a reputable exchange is fine. The risk is proportionate. But if you're holding meaningful amounts — anything you'd genuinely feel sick about losing — a hardware wallet stops being a luxury and starts being a necessity."

"From my perspective as someone who works in compliance: the exchanges are getting safer. Regulation is improving. But the history of crypto is littered with exchanges that seemed solid right until they weren't. Mt. Gox. Bitfinex. Celsius. FTX. Self-custody is still the safest option. €80 is a cheap insurance policy."

📱 TEXT: "€80 = the cheapest insurance policy in crypto."

"If you want to see the step-by-step Ledger setup — full tutorial — I've written it all up at floronchain.com. Link in bio. And if this was useful, subscribe — I post one video a week on crypto, compliance, and how to actually navigate this space safely. See you next week."

📱 TEXT: "floronchain.com · Subscribe for weekly crypto education"

What Are AI Agent Tokens? The Newest Thing in Crypto — Explained Simply
8–11 min Beginners + Curious professionals Miles Deutscher style
SEO
Primary keyword: AI agent tokens explained · Secondary: what are AI agents crypto, Virtuals Protocol explained, ai16z token, AI crypto 2025, autonomous AI crypto agents · Intent: Informational (trending)
FULL SCRIPT
HOOK — 0:00–0:10 · Direct to camera · Slight edge of excitement in voice

"What if there was a crypto token backed by an AI that trades on your behalf, manages a portfolio, runs its own social media account, and operates 24 hours a day — completely autonomously. No human in the loop."

[pause]

"That's not science fiction. That's happening right now. It's called an AI agent token. And it's the newest, most controversial thing in crypto."

📱 TEXT: "AI Agent Tokens — the most controversial thing in crypto right now"

WHAT IS AN AI AGENT — 0:10–2:00 · Camera + simple graphic if possible

"Okay, let me back up. What actually is an AI agent?"

"An AI agent is a software program that can take actions autonomously. It's not just answering questions like ChatGPT — it can execute tasks. It can browse the internet, interact with other software, make decisions, and act on them. Without a human telling it what to do step by step."

"Think about it like this. ChatGPT is like a really smart assistant that answers your questions when you ask. An AI agent is like hiring that same assistant, giving them a credit card, and saying: 'Go handle my crypto portfolio. Make good decisions. Report back.' And then — they actually do it."

📱 TEXT: "AI Agent = takes actions autonomously. No human required."

AI AGENTS IN CRYPTO — 2:00–3:30 · Camera

"In crypto, AI agents are being built to do things like: manage DeFi yield strategies automatically, trade tokens based on market signals, post on X and build audiences to drive token value, conduct smart contract audits, and even negotiate with other AI agents on your behalf."

"The most famous early example was an AI agent called AIXBT. It analysed crypto market data and posted insights on X. It built a real following. And it had its own token. At peak, that token was worth over $800 million."

📱 TEXT: "AIXBT: AI-run crypto analyst. $800M peak market cap."

WHAT IS AN AI AGENT TOKEN — 3:30–5:00 · Camera

"So where does the token come in? An AI agent token is basically the economic layer around an AI agent. You buy the token, and in return you might get: revenue sharing from what the agent earns, governance rights over how the agent behaves, early access to the agent's services, or simply exposure to whether people think that agent will become valuable."

"The biggest platform for this right now is called Virtuals Protocol, which runs on Base — Coinbase's Layer 2. It lets anyone launch an AI agent with a token. Think of it like Pump.fun but for AI agents instead of meme coins."

📱 TEXT: "Virtuals Protocol — launch your own AI agent token"

"Another major one is ai16z — an AI-managed venture capital fund on Solana. Genuinely run by an AI agent that decides what to invest in. At peak it had a $2 billion market cap."

📱 TEXT: "ai16z: AI-run VC fund. $2B market cap at peak."

IS IT REAL OR IS IT HYPE — 5:00–7:00 · Camera · Direct, honest

"Okay, here's where I'm going to be straight with you, because I think a lot of coverage of this topic is either pure hype or pure dismissal. The truth is somewhere more interesting."

"The underlying technology is real. AI agents that can execute onchain transactions autonomously — that's genuinely new and genuinely powerful. The ability to create economic primitives around AI behaviour is a legitimately novel idea."

"But. The current market for AI agent tokens is 95% speculation. Most of these tokens have no revenue, no real utility, and no moat. They're betting that a particular AI agent will become dominant — and most won't. Just like 99% of DeFi tokens in 2021 went to zero, most AI agent tokens will too."

📱 TEXT: "Real tech. But 95% speculation. Most will go to zero."

"The question to ask about any AI agent token is: does this agent actually do something that creates value? Real, measurable value? Or is the token just a bet that other people will buy it later? Those are very different things."

THE COMPLIANCE ANGLE — 7:00–8:30 · Camera · Your unique lens

"Now here's the angle no one else is talking about — and this is where it gets genuinely fascinating from my side of the table."

"If an AI agent executes a financial transaction — buys a token, moves funds across chains, interacts with a mixer — who is responsible for that transaction? Legally? From an AML perspective?"

"Right now, there is no clear answer. Current AML regulation is built around the assumption that a human being made a decision. AI agents break that assumption completely. The FATF hasn't published guidance on autonomous agents. The EU's AI Act and MiCA don't fully address this overlap. It's a regulatory gap — and as these agents get more capable, it's one that's going to become impossible to ignore."

📱 TEXT: "Who's responsible when an AI agent launders money? Nobody knows yet."

"I'm watching this space very closely because I think the first enforcement action involving an AI agent executing suspicious transactions is going to be a landmark moment — and it's probably coming within 2 years."

FLOR'S TAKE — 8:30–9:30 · Camera · Honest, no hype

"My honest take? I'm more excited about the underlying technology than I am about any specific token right now."

"The idea of AI agents that can autonomously execute complex financial strategies is genuinely transformative — for DeFi, for compliance automation, for portfolio management. But the tokens that are trading right now are mostly speculation on future value that may or may not materialise."

"If you want exposure to this space, the smarter play is probably the platforms — Virtuals Protocol, the infrastructure layer — not individual agent tokens. And as always with new sectors: small position sizes, and money you can afford to lose."

📱 TEXT: "My take: exciting technology. Speculative tokens. Small positions only."
